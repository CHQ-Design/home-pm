## Plenum — CUT

### Cultural connotations: WRONG TEMPERAMENT

"Plenum" is a real English word but carries connotations that are off-brand for a warm household app:

- **Soviet/Communist political body.** A "plenum" specifically refers to a meeting of all members of a Communist Party committee — the Central Committee Plenum is iconic in 20th-century history. The 1964 plenum removed Khrushchev. To anyone with passing knowledge of 20th-century history, "Plenum" reads as Politburo-coded.
- **HVAC industrial.** "Plenum chamber" is the technical term for the air-distribution box in ventilation systems. "Plenum-rated cable" is a networking-cable standard.
- **Bureaucratic assembly.** General English usage is for formal legislative or committee meetings where all members are present.

For a home-pm brand whose entire positioning is *the grounded, calm household*, these connotations actively work against the product. Cold, bureaucratic, industrial, authoritarian.

### Brand and domain: HEAVILY CROWDED

- **plenumapps.com** — taken by Plenum HSE Software (health, safety, environment workplace productivity software). This is the natural URL pattern for us; it's gone.
- **Vivid by Plenum** — iOS app for HSE professionals on Apple App Store, classified as productivity tool
- **Plenum AI** (plenum-ai.com) — Middle East AI/ERP/cloud solutions
- **plenum AG** (plenum.de) — German management consulting firm
- **Plenum Tech** (plenum-tech.com) — Middle East tech company

Multiple active enterprise/business software brands using "Plenum." Trademark space is congested in Class 9 / 42.

### Verdict: **DROP.** Connotations alone disqualify before brand crowding does.

## Hovi — SIGNIFICANTLY COMPROMISED

### Slang screen: PROBLEMATIC

Urban Dictionary's primary entry defines **"hovi" as slang for Jehovah's Witnesses**. Related entries in the same orbit:

- **"Hov"** — Jay-Z's nickname, shortened from "Jay-Hova," a play on "Jehovah." Carries strong rap-cultural association.
- **"Hovis"** — major UK bread brand. The brand's 1970s marketing also got picked up as racially-coded slang for "mixed-race person with black father and white mother."

None of these are hard stops individually, but together they pull the brand in directions you didn't intend: religion, rap culture, UK bread, racial coding. Every user who Googles "Hovi" hits at least one of these associations before they hit the product.

### Brand landscape: CROWDED IN ADJACENT SOFTWARE

Multiple active tech/software brands using "Hovi":

- **Hovi Digital Lab** (thehovi.com) — Dubai-based AI marketing agency, 50+ specialists, 200+ brands since 2019. Capterra classifies them in **Business/Productivity Software**. This is an adjacent category.
- **Hovi** (hovi.id) — identity/credentials platform for verifiable credentials and wallets
- **Hovi Studio** (Capterra-listed software)
- **Hovi Technologies, Inc.** — owns trademarks; explicitly requires written consent for any "Hovi" trademark use
- **Hovi.ai** — app.hovi.ai is the credentials platform app

### Domain landscape: NO CLEAN PRIMARY URL

- **hovi.ai** — taken (Hovi credentials platform)
- **hovi.id** — taken (Hovi credentials)
- **thehovi.com** — taken (Hovi Digital Lab)
- **hovi.com** — unclear status, likely registered
- Available patterns would be contorted: usehovi.com, hovihouse.com, etc.

### Verdict: **DROP.**

The combination of (a) Jehovah's Witnesses slang on Urban Dictionary, (b) Hov/Jay-Z association, (c) three+ active tech brands including productivity-adjacent Hovi Digital Lab, and (d) no clean primary URL makes Hovi noisier than Cadence. Cut it.

## Otium — SURVIVABLE WITH CAVEATS

### Slang screen: CLEAN

No English slang associations. Otium is a Latin word with positive Roman-intellectual heritage — productive leisure / contemplative quiet time, contrasted historically with *negotium* (active work/business). The concept has been picked up by intellectual-Twitter productivity-philosophy writers (David Perell, others) which is a slight pre-claim but a positive one.

### Pronunciation: TRAINING NEEDED

Otium is pronounced **OH-tee-um** (English approximation of Latin OH-tee-oom). This is non-obvious to English speakers. Equivalent risk profile to what Toft would have needed: an onboarding pronunciation note. Manageable but not free.

### Brand landscape: BUSY BUT NOT DIRECT-COMPETITIVE

Multiple Otium-branded products exist, but **none in direct household-management competition**:

- **Otium Regulatory DocWriter™** (otium.digital) — medical/regulatory writing software, trademarked. Active.
- **Otium Services** — commercial cleaning app for hotels/hospitality
- **Otium Soft** (otiumsoft.com) — separate software company
- **Otium ERP / OtiumMobile** (otiumobile.com) — mobile devices / lifestyle / ERP
- **Otium Business Consulting** (otiumbc.com)
- **Otium Facilita** — Google Play app (Brazilian)
- **Otium app** (otium-app.github.io) — small indie focus/mindfulness app. **This is the closest adjacent product** — it's a distraction-blocker. Different from household management but in the same broad "calm productivity" emotional space. Worth noting; not a hard stop since it's a small github.io project rather than a commercial competitor.
- **Otium Restaurant LA** — Chef Timothy Hollingsworth's well-known DTLA restaurant near The Broad. **Now CLOSED per Yelp (May 2026 update)** — this frees up significant brand-space and SEO that was previously dominated by the restaurant.

The trademark situation requires attorney work — Otium Regulatory holds a trademark in their category (medical/regulatory writing software), but coexistence on different goods/services in Class 9 / 42 is plausible. Not a slam dunk; not a blocker.

### Domain landscape: ACQUIRABLE BUT EXPENSIVE

- **otium.com** — listed for sale (price unknown, likely $5K–$25K based on premium one-word .com market). The owner uses identity-hiding service. Acquisition not guaranteed and likely meaningful spend.
- **otium.io** — listed for sale on Namoxy domain marketplace (premium domain pricing)
- **otium.app** — status unclear, requires direct registrar check (per v2 lesson: who.is unreliable for .app)
- **otium.digital** — taken (Otium Regulatory)
- **otium.cc** — parked at DreamHost
- **otiumsoft.com / otiumobile.com / otiumbc.com / thehovi.com etc.** — taken by various Otium brands

**Realistic options:**
1. Acquire otium.com if budget allows ($5K–$25K range, broker negotiation required)
2. Use **getotium.com** or **useotium.com** as primary URL (Stripe/Notion playbook)
3. Try otium.app at registrar (verify, don't trust who.is)

### Verdict: **THE STRONGEST CANDIDATE OF THE THREE.**

Otium is not without compromise — no name in this exercise has been. But:

- **No direct same-category competitor exists** (unlike Tend/Tend Task, or Stead/stead.space)
- **No slang or cultural collision** (unlike Toft/toff or Hovi/JW)
- **The most prominent Otium brand (LA restaurant) just closed**, freeing up SEO/cultural namespace
- **The Latin origin gives the name a story to tell** — Otium specifically means "productive leisure" / "the good quiet hours when meaningful things happen." This is almost exactly what home-pm is trying to enable for households.
- **The pronunciation note is manageable** — same lesson Toft was going to teach in onboarding, just for different reasons

The cost of choosing Otium:
1. An onboarding pronunciation guide ("Otium — say OH-tee-um")
2. Probable $5K–$25K for otium.com (or settle for getotium.com)
3. Attorney coexistence work with Otium Regulatory
4. Carrying the (small) Otium-app github.io project as background noise

The value of choosing Otium:
1. A name with genuine etymological substance — the most defensible "old word, on purpose" story since Toft
2. No collision with a direct competitor
3. Aligns precisely with the calm-productivity positioning
4. Distinctive in the household-app category (no other Latin-borrowing brand)
5. Fraunces serif typography would land beautifully on the word
