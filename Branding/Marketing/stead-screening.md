# Stead — Preliminary Screening Result

_Prepared: 2026-05-19. Preliminary screening only, not legal advice. A proper attorney-led trademark search is still required before any commercial launch under any name._

## Verdict: **Disqualified — direct category collision**

A live closed-beta product called **Stead** already exists at `stead.space`, built in Bristol, UK, in the same product category as home-pm (household-management / home-admin app). Surfaced via web search and confirmed by fetching the product's own marketing site.

## Evidence

**Product name and positioning:** _Stead_ — tagline _"Your home, properly organised."_ Self-described as the "UK home operating system". Source: `stead.space/features` and `stead.space` (Open Graph metadata: `meta-og:site_name: Stead`, `meta-og:image:alt: Stead, the UK home operating system`).

**Status:** Closed beta as of 2026, with documented plans for App Store and Google Play launch following a public open beta, and active development through "early 2027". Source: `stead.space/features` ("Coming soon" section). This places their commercial-launch window inside the same 6–12 month runway home-pm is operating against.

**Category overlap:** The feature set overlaps materially with home-pm's planned scope:

- Recurring maintenance tasks with cadence ("Tasks that recur the way real life does")
- Document vault with expiry warnings (warranties, certificates — the same use case as home-pm's planned Notes)
- AI diagnostics / property-aware troubleshooting
- Multi-mode (homeowner / renter / landlord) — analogous to home-pm's admin/member/kid modes
- Energy and bills tracking

UK-specific elements (EPC lookup, gov.uk certificate integration, postcode search via Postcodes.io) are not in home-pm's scope, but the _category_ — "the app that runs your household" — is identical.

**Brand surface already claimed:** From the `stead.space` footer, the company has active handles on X (`@SteadApp`), Instagram (`@stead.app`), TikTok (`@steadapp`), Threads (`@stead.app`), Bluesky (`steadapp.bsky.social`), and YouTube (`@SteadApp`). The `stead.app` social handles in particular are occupied — meaning even US-only social handles for "Stead" are already cross-posted by an active competitor.

**Jurisdictional note:** Stead (Bristol) is UK-targeted; home-pm is presumably US-targeted. Trademarks are territorial, so in theory US and UK marks could be filed independently. In practice, app stores, web search, and social platforms are global. A US user searching "stead app" in 2027 will overwhelmingly find Stead (Bristol). Coexistence is hostile, not neutral.

## What screening did _not_ check (no longer relevant)

The original screening plan also covered USPTO TESS live-mark lookup, domain WHOIS for `stead.app` / `stead.com` / `stead.family` / `stead.house` / etc., and US social-handle availability. None of these were completed, because the category-level collision above is itself disqualifying. Even if `stead.app` the domain were available and no US trademark were yet filed, launching a household-management app called Stead in 2026–2027 means launching directly into another product's growing brand presence in the same category.

## Recommendation

**Treat Stead as ruled out for home-pm under any commercial-launch scenario.** Pivot screening to the next two candidates from the readout:

1. **Mantel** — the warmest-emotional-fit option. Watch the mantel/mantle spelling collision and run a full USPTO + domain screen.
2. **Folio** — the editorial-fit option. Heavy collision risk in financial/portfolio software space; screening will surface whether the household-app space is open.

If both fail screening too, the readout's remaining candidates (Roost, Tally, Nook, Around Here, Kindling) should be screened in order.

## Caveat

This screen relied on public web evidence only. It did not include USPTO TESS, UKIPO, EUIPO, or common-law searches by a trademark attorney, and is not a substitute for one. The finding above is strong enough on its face to disqualify Stead without needing an attorney to confirm — _the product already exists and is shipping_ — but the inverse (a name passing this screen) would not be sufficient to clear it for commercial use.
