# Tend + Cadence — Screening Report

**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Status:** Screening complete. **Both candidates fail.** Tend is a hard stop. Cadence is survivable but significantly compromised.
**Prior work:** [naming-v3.md](naming-v3.md), [home-pm-branding-direction memory](../../../Library/Application%20Support/Claude/local-agent-mode-sessions/...). [Stead screening](stead-screening.md) is the direct precedent for the Tend finding.

## Methodology

Three-dimension screen per the v3 doc:

1. **Slang and cultural** — Urban Dictionary + general slang dictionaries (US/UK/AU)
2. **Brand and trademark landscape** — direct competitors and major trademark holders
3. **Domain and social handle availability** — paused after dimension 2 disqualified both

This is the screening pass that **was skipped before Toft was committed on 2026-05-19**. The lesson from Toft: do not commit before this screen runs.

## Tend

### Slang screen: CLEAN

No problematic slang associations for "tend" as a verb or noun. Adjacent terms found ("tendies" = chicken tenders / WallStreetBets gains slang; "tender" = soft/emotional, also attractive) do not apply to the brand name "Tend" itself. No British, American, or Australian slang collisions found.

### Brand landscape: **HARD STOP**

**Tend Task: Household Organizer** is a direct same-category competitor, actively maintained through at least February 2026:

- Live on Apple App Store (ID 1456102211, launched 2019) and Google Play
- Owned by Tend Task, Inc. (founder: Felicia Kashevaroff, relationship coach)
- Owns domains **tend.house** and **tendtask.com**
- Product description verbatim matches home-pm's territory: *"Build a team by inviting your partner, roommates, kids, extended family… create custom lists that function for your unique household."*
- Has an AI feature called Harmony
- Added a Wedding Planning category in a recent update

This is the exact pattern that killed Stead on 2026-05-19 (the stead.space UK home-admin app). Filing a USPTO trademark application for "Tend" in Class 9 / 42 would almost certainly be opposed. App Store search would return both products. SEO would be permanently muddied. User reviews and word-of-mouth would conflate them.

**Tend Dental** (Noho Dental, Inc.) is an additional but secondary concern:

- USPTO trademark registration 6185750 for TEND DENTAL
- 14 trademarks owned across dental products and services
- Operates in NYC, DC, Boston, Atlanta, Nashville with 100K+ patients
- Owns hellotend.com
- Different category (dental services, Class 35/44 and toothbrush goods Class 21) but the TEND wordmark in commerce

**Other "Tend" branded products** (creating SEO/brand noise):

- Tend.global (separate company, Trustpilot presence)
- Tending.app (separate product)
- Tend Smart Farm / Tend Companion App (agricultural)
- Tend Hospitality App
- Multiple smaller Tend-branded products

### Verdict: **DROP**

This is not survivable. A live, actively-maintained, same-category competitor with the exact same name is a hard stop. Do not re-propose Tend.

## Cadence

### Slang screen: CLEAN

No problematic slang associations. Urban Dictionary entries are dominated by descriptions of "Cadence" as a girl's first name (positive personality descriptions) and the standard musical/rhythmic meaning. Worth noting that **Cadence is a popular girl's name in the US**, which is a soft brand consideration — the name carries human-warm associations but may create "the app or the person?" confusion in some contexts.

### Brand landscape: HEAVILY CROWDED

**Cadence Design Systems** is the trademark goliath:

- Multi-billion-dollar chip design / EDA company (NASDAQ: CDNS)
- Owns the CADENCE wordmark for "computer software and hardware for the design, testing, fabrication, and installation of integrated circuits, semiconductors, and printed circuit boards"
- Different vertical (chip design) but Class 9 overlap
- Will dominate every USPTO TESS search hit for "Cadence" in software classes
- An attorney would need to argue coexistence based on product distinctness

**Active Cadence-branded software products** (creating brand confusion):

- **Cadence Deep Work App** (iOS productivity, recent launch) — adjacent productivity space, focuses on focus sessions
- **Cadence App** (food delivery, Jersey) — owns **cadenceapp.com**, would be the natural URL pattern for us
- **Cadence by Ringover** (sales prospecting automation)
- **Cadence** (event management app)
- **Cadence workflow engine** (open-source, GitHub: cadence-workflow/cadence)
- **Cadence** (bike/run tracker, Google Play, com.sevenbold.cadence)
- **Cadence: Daily Task Planner** (Amazon-published productivity journal)
- **Cadence Productivity SaaS** (Framer template — branding pattern)

### Domain landscape (spot check)

- **cadence.com** — Cadence Design Systems. Not available.
- **cadenceapp.com** — Cadence App food delivery (Jersey). Not available.
- **cadence.app** — appears to be a domain registration / parked page; status unclear, requires registrar check.
- **getcadence.app** — actively in use by a third party.

The clean URL patterns are gone. Available patterns would be more contorted: usecadence.com, cadencehouse.com, etc.

### Verdict: **NOT DEAD, BUT SIGNIFICANTLY COMPROMISED**

Cadence is not in the hard-stop position Tend is — none of the existing products are direct household-management competitors. But:

1. The trademark goliath (Cadence Design Systems) makes Class 9 filing complicated and expensive
2. The cleanest URLs (cadence.com, cadenceapp.com) are taken — including by another app explicitly branded "Cadence App"
3. The productivity-app namespace is already busy with Cadence Deep Work
4. SEO and App Store search will be polluted from day one

Cadence could ship, but it would be fighting noise indefinitely. Not a confident foundation for a 6–12 month commercial launch path.

## Pattern recognition: why this keeps happening

This is the **fourth** name in the home-pm naming process to fall to screening:

| Name | Status | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Stead | Out | stead.space — direct same-category competitor in UK |
| Toft | Out | "toff" — British class slang collision |
| Tend | Out | Tend Task — direct same-category competitor + Tend Dental trademark |
| Cadence | Compromised | Cadence Design Systems + Cadence App (food delivery) + Cadence Deep Work + many more |

**The pattern:** common, short, warm-or-productivity English words for household / domestic / care concepts are systematically claimed in 2026. The household-task-management app category specifically has consumed the cleanest names. The screening cost (an hour of search) keeps revealing what 30 seconds of trademark search would have caught — but only if pointed at the right names.

**The lesson:** the whitespace is no longer in "common English real-word verbs." It's in:

1. **Less common real words** (Toft would have worked except for the slang collision)
2. **Compound words** (Daybook, Throughline — both already on the v3 list)
3. **Light coinages** (Toftly was the v2 alternative; Craig found it thin but it solved this problem)
4. **Two-word constructions** (Around Here, Common House from v2)

The v3 list still has candidates that are likely cleaner than Tend or Cadence: **Stride, Knit, Spry, Daybook, Throughline.** Each would need its own screening pass. None has been verified.

## Recommendations

### Immediate

1. **Drop Tend.** Do not re-propose. Add to the "ruled out" list alongside Stead, Toft, Doable, Heyday.
2. **Hold Cadence as a viable-but-noisy fallback,** not as a confident primary.
3. **Pause domain/handle acquisition.** No purchases until a primary candidate is locked.

### Strategic options

**Option A — Accept Cadence and proceed.** Cheapest path. Accepts permanent SEO noise and attorney complexity. Defensible but never clean.

**Option B — Screen the next-best v3 candidates: Stride, Spry, Daybook, Throughline, Knit.** One of these may turn out to be the right answer. ~1 hour of focused screening per name.

**Option C — Generate a fresh v3.5 list with smarter constraints:** explicitly avoid (a) common single-syllable warm-verbs (the saturated lane), (b) anything previously ruled out, (c) anything in the home/hearth metaphor space. Lean into compounds, less-common real words, and light coinages that the v3 strategy under-weighted. Then screen the top 2–3.

**My recommendation: B then C.** Stride and Daybook in particular are worth screening before generating new options — they were in the v3 list specifically because they sat outside the single-syllable warm-verb lane that's failing. If both fail, v3.5 is justified. If one survives, we save the work.
