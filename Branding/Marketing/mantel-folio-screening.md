# Mantel & Folio — Preliminary Screening Results

_Prepared: 2026-05-19. Preliminary screening only, not legal advice. A proper attorney-led trademark search is still required before any commercial launch under any name._

## Verdicts

**Mantel — high risk, probably unusable.** A high-profile consumer app called **Mantel** already exists in the App Store and Google Play (a collectibles social network for sports cards, TCGs, comics, coins, sneakers, watches), founded by Alexis Ohanian (Reddit), Brent Montgomery (_Pawn Stars_ producer), and Evan Parker (formerly of The Athletic), with the mobile app launched in December 2024. Different category from home-pm, but consumer-app surface, established trademark interest, and aggressive brand defense are all but certain. The fact that they registered under `onmantel.com` strongly suggests `mantel.com` was already unavailable when they launched.

**Folio — dead. Do not pursue.** "Folio" is one of the most heavily saturated product names in current consumer and SaaS software. At least six active products use the name across categories that directly or adjacently overlap with home-pm.

## Mantel — evidence

**Mantel (collectibles social network) — `onmantel.com`.** Live iOS app at `apps.apple.com/us/app/mantel-collect-share-connect/id6737240833`. Live Android app at `play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.onmantel.app`. Launched December 2024 with TechCrunch coverage. Founders include Reddit co-founder Alexis Ohanian, _Pawn Stars_ producer Brent Montgomery, and former The Athletic GM Evan Parker. The brand has significant celebrity-founder backing and a clear path to scaling — a US-based household-management app called "Mantel" will fight this product for SEO, app-store search, and trademark space in the consumer-app category.

**Mantle (SaaS billing platform) — `heymantle.com`.** Homophonic spelling. SaaS subscription-management platform. Not a direct collision in category, but adds noise and increases the likelihood of users typing the wrong domain or searching the wrong term.

**Mantle (cap table software) — `withmantle.com`.** Homophonic spelling. Different category. Adds further spelling-confusion noise.

**Domain signal.** That two separate active companies use `heymantle.com` and `withmantle.com` (rather than `mantle.com`) and that the consumer Mantel app uses `onmantel.com` (rather than `mantel.com`) is a strong signal that the bare `mantel.com` and `mantle.com` domains are taken or held by someone unwilling to sell at a reasonable price. Premium-domain cost for `mantel.app` or `mantel.com` is likely high.

## Folio — evidence

**Folio: Digital Wallet App — `folioltd`.** Live iOS app at `apps.apple.com/us/app/folio-digital-wallet-app/id1266382717` and Google Play. Organizes IDs, loyalty cards, credit cards, gift cards, tickets. **This is in the adjacent "organize your stuff" category** even though it's documents-not-tasks. Direct app-store collision.

**Folio: Save Now Read Later — `savewithfolio.com`.** Live iOS app at `apps.apple.com/us/app/folio-save-now-read-later/id6746292066`. Read-it-later category, but consumer app named Folio with current app store presence.

**Folio (project finance diligence) — `getfolio.io`.** B2B finance SaaS, not a consumer collision but uses the `getfolio` URL pattern recommended in the original readout.

**FOLIO (open-source library management) — `folio.org`.** Long-running open-source project hosted at `github.com/folio-org`. Owns the `.org` and significant GitHub mindshare.

**Folio (contracts / risk / compliance) — `usefolio.com`.** B2B SaaS using the `usefolio` URL pattern, also recommended in the original readout.

**Folio Document Organizer — `savor.us/products/the-folio-document-organizer`.** Physical organizer product marketed as a "life binder" for estate plans, passports, financial records. **Registers their mark as "The Folio™"** — meaning trademark interest is asserted in the "organize your life" consumer-products space.

**folio.id.** Live consumer product with Trustpilot reviews. Category unclear from search but actively in market.

Net: every recommended URL pattern from the original readout (`folio.app`, `getfolio.com`, `usefolio.com`) is in use by an active product, plus two App Store consumer apps named Folio are live, plus a registered "The Folio™" trademark sits adjacent to home-pm's category. **There is no defensible space left for a household-management app called Folio.**

## What the three screens together tell us

Three candidates screened (Stead, Mantel, Folio), three significant collisions found. The pattern is informative: **short, evocative, single-word English nouns aimed at the home/household metaphor space are systematically claimed in 2026.** Stead was a same-category direct competitor; Mantel is a celebrity-founded consumer app with App Store presence; Folio is saturated across at least six active products. The candidate-generation approach in the original readout — short evocative metaphor-anchored nouns — under-weighted the defensibility cost.

This is a useful negative result, not a failure. We know now:

- **The household-metaphor noun space is mined out.** Hearth, Stead, Mantel, Roost, Nook, Folio, Tally, Ledger — all likely taken or contested.
- **The defensibility bar should be the primary filter** in the next pass, not an afterthought.
- **The next candidate list needs to include name types we excluded the first time**: compound words, coinages, modifier-plus-noun phrases, made-up words.

## Recommendation

Three paths from here, in order of how strongly I'd recommend each:

1. **Generate a new candidate list with defensibility as the lead filter.** Re-do the naming pass with explicit constraints: avoid bare evocative English nouns; include compounds (e.g. _Around Here_, _Hometable_, _Stoop_), coinages (Slack/Notion/Asana style), and modifier-plus-noun phrases. Screen the top 3 from the new list before any commitment. This is the highest-EV move; the negative result from the three screens is genuinely informative about what _kind_ of name to look for.

2. **Screen down the second tier** (Roost, Tally, Nook, Around Here, Kindling) before regenerating. Faster than option 1 but lower expected value — based on the pattern so far, Roost / Tally / Nook will probably also collide.

3. **Pursue Mantel anyway**, on the bet that the category difference (collectibles vs household) is enough separation. Plausible but risky: the collectibles Mantel has celebrity founders, App Store presence, and momentum; coexistence in consumer-app search and trademark space is a fight, not a given. I would not recommend this without an attorney's read first.

## Caveat

This screen relied on public web evidence only. It did not include USPTO TESS, UKIPO, EUIPO, or common-law searches by a trademark attorney. The findings above are strong enough on their face to disqualify Folio outright and to make Mantel high-risk; any final naming decision still needs a proper attorney pass before commercial use.
